### Step 1: Define the problem
Identify the problem you are trying to solve in a deterministic manner
### Step 2: Determine how you are going to solve the problem
This is the step where you determine how you are going to solve the problem defined in step 1. Being thoughtful and attentive during this step can help you save a lot of time in the later stages of developing a program. It is too common to start programming before conceptually coming up with a solution. Good solutions have the following characteristics:
- They are straightforward (not overly complicated or confusing)
- They are well documented (especially around any assumptions being made or limitations)
- They are build modularly, so parts can be reused or changed later without impacting other parts of the program
- The can recover gracefully or give useful error messages when something unexpected happens
